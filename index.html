What I do

Workshops for companies and teams

  • Communication and active listening
  • Collaboration and trust
  • Leadership and status dynamics
  • Dealing with uncertainty and change
  • Presence and clarity in interactions

Improvisation makes visible the dynamics that often remain implicit in working environments, creating a concrete space to observe, understand, and transform them.

Workshops for groups and wellbeing

  • Relational awareness
  • Trust and group connection
  • Presence and body awareness
  • Exploring relationships with others and with the unexpected

The work is experiential, accessible, and respectful of each participant’s pace and boundaries.

My approach

  • Experience before theory: learning happens through doing, observing, and reflecting together
  • Structure and safety: play only works within clear and shared boundaries
  • Listening and relationship: attention is always on what happens between people
  • No performance required: there is no need to “act” or perform

Improvisation is not used as entertainment, but as a tool for inquiry and growth.

Who I am

I am a trainer and teacher of theatrical improvisation.

Over the years I have worked with a wide variety of groups: corporate teams, artists, students, and people with no previous theatre experience. This has allowed me to develop a solid, flexible, and deeply human approach.

I collaborate with an international network of professionals and training organisations, integrating artistic, pedagogical, and organisational expertise.

My goal is not to teach techniques, but to create the conditions for a group to function better.
